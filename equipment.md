<div class="container" style="padding:72px 0;">
  <h1 class="pageTitle">MESLO — Equipment</h1>
  <p class="lead">Control equipment costs. Align production. Protect margin.</p>

  <p>Equipment is rarely the issue—misalignment is. When usage, cost, and production aren’t tied together, equipment quietly erodes margin.</p>
  <p>MESLO treats equipment as a tracked, measurable driver of output, not a flat cost.</p>

  <h2>What Equipment Covers</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Owned equipment (depreciation, fuel, maintenance)</li>
    <li>Rented equipment (rates, duration, terms)</li>
    <li>Mobilization and setup</li>
    <li>Operator coordination with labor</li>
    <li>Utilization and idle time</li>
    <li>Production capacity and constraints</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Why It Matters</h2>
  <p>When equipment is not structured:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Costs are underestimated at bid stage</li>
    <li>Idle time reduces profitability</li>
    <li>Production assumptions don’t match reality</li>
    <li>Schedules slip and coordination breaks down</li>
  </ul>
  <p>A structured system ensures:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Costs reflect actual usage</li>
    <li>Production aligns with estimating</li>
    <li>Equipment supports execution—not delays it</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Common Failure Points</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Flat rates applied without usage tracking</li>
    <li>Ignoring idle or partial utilization</li>
    <li>Missing mobilization and setup costs</li>
    <li>No connection between equipment and output</li>
    <li>Treating equipment as overhead instead of job cost</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>What a Structured System Looks Like</h2>
  <p>A MESLO-aligned equipment system includes:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Defined usage assumptions tied to scope and schedule</li>
    <li>Clear cost structure (owned vs rented)</li>
    <li>Utilization tracking across job phases</li>
    <li>Mobilization costs captured upfront</li>
    <li>Production rates aligned with equipment capacity</li>
  </ul>
  <p>This creates a system where:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>usage is predictable</li>
    <li>costs are visible</li>
    <li>output is measurable</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>For CPAs and Advisors</h2>
  <p>Equipment is a primary source of cost variance.</p>
  <p>A structured approach allows you to:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Validate equipment costs against actual usage</li>
    <li>Identify idle or underutilized assets</li>
    <li>Separate job costs from overhead allocation</li>
    <li>Improve forecasting tied to production capacity</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>How It Connects to MESLO</h2>
  <p>Equipment directly impacts:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Labor → crew size and operator needs</li>
    <li>Materials → handling and installation</li>
    <li>Subcontractors → coordination and scope overlap</li>
    <li>Schedule & margin → speed and efficiency</li>
  </ul>
  <p>If equipment assumptions are off, production and margin follow.</p>

  <h2>Outcome</h2>
  <p>When equipment is structured correctly:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Production matches estimating assumptions</li>
    <li>Costs reflect real usage</li>
    <li>Idle time is reduced</li>
    <li>Margins become predictable</li>
  </ul>
