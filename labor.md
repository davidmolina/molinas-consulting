<div class="container" style="padding:72px 0;">
  <h1 class="pageTitle">MESLO — Labor</h1>
  <p class="lead">Align production. Control labor cost. Protect margin.</p>

  <p>Labor is where estimates meet reality. If production assumptions are off, labor becomes the fastest way to lose margin.</p>
  <p>MESLO treats labor as a measured production system, not just hourly cost.</p>

  <h2>What “Labor” Covers</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Crew composition and size</li>
    <li>Hourly rates (burdened wages, taxes, benefits)</li>
    <li>Production rates (units per hour/day)</li>
    <li>Job duration and sequencing</li>
    <li>Overtime and schedule pressure</li>
    <li>Rework and inefficiency risk</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Why It Matters</h2>
  <p>When labor is not structured:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Production assumptions don’t match actual output</li>
    <li>Hours are underestimated at bid stage</li>
    <li>Overtime erodes margin</li>
    <li>Crews are overstaffed or underutilized</li>
    <li>Schedules slip and costs increase</li>
  </ul>
  <p>A structured system ensures:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Labor hours align with real production</li>
    <li>Rates reflect true cost</li>
    <li>Crew sizing matches the scope</li>
    <li>Execution stays consistent with the estimate</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Common Failure Points</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Using “rule of thumb” hours instead of production-based estimates</li>
    <li>Ignoring burden (taxes, insurance, benefits)</li>
    <li>No linkage between scope quantities and labor hours</li>
    <li>Not tracking actual vs estimated production</li>
    <li>Treating labor as a flat cost instead of a variable tied to output</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>What a Structured System Looks Like</h2>
  <p>A MESLO-aligned labor system includes:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Defined production rates tied to scope</li>
    <li>Crew structure aligned with task requirements</li>
    <li>Fully burdened rates (wages + taxes + insurance)</li>
    <li>Hours calculated from output, not guesswork</li>
    <li>Tracking of actual vs estimated performance</li>
  </ul>
  <p>This creates a system where:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>production is measurable</li>
    <li>labor cost is predictable</li>
    <li>performance can be improved over time</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>For CPAs and Advisors</h2>
  <p>Labor is one of the largest and most variable cost drivers.</p>
  <p>A structured approach allows you to:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Compare estimated vs actual labor performance</li>
    <li>Identify inefficiencies and cost overruns</li>
    <li>Validate burdened labor rates</li>
    <li>Improve forecasting tied to production</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>How It Connects to MESLO</h2>
  <p>Labor directly impacts:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Materials → installation pace and waste</li>
    <li>Equipment → operator needs and productivity</li>
    <li>Subcontractors → scope overlap and coordination</li>
    <li>Overhead & margin → total job profitability</li>
  </ul>
  <p>If labor assumptions are wrong, the entire estimate is compromised.</p>

  <h2>Outcome</h2>
  <p>When labor is structured correctly:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Production matches estimating assumptions</li>
    <li>Labor cost reflects real output</li>
    <li>Overtime and inefficiencies are reduced</li>
    <li>Margins become more stable</li>
  </ul>
