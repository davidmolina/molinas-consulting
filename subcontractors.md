<div class="container" style="padding:72px 0;">
  <h1 class="pageTitle">MESLO — Subcontractors</h1>
  <p class="lead">Clarify scope. Normalize bids. Protect margin.</p>

  <p>Subcontractor pricing often looks competitive—but hides risk. When scope, assumptions, and inclusions are not aligned, the lowest bid becomes the most expensive.</p>
  <p>MESLO treats subcontractors as a structured input, not a plug-in number.</p>

  <h2>What “Subcontractors” Covers</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Trade scope definition (what is included vs excluded)</li>
    <li>Bid leveling and comparison across vendors</li>
    <li>Unit pricing vs lump sum proposals</li>
    <li>Schedule coordination and sequencing</li>
    <li>Change order exposure and gap risk</li>
    <li>Vendor reliability and performance history</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Why It Matters</h2>
  <p>When subcontractors are not structured:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Bids are not comparable</li>
    <li>Scope gaps create change orders</li>
    <li>Pricing looks lower than it actually is</li>
    <li>Coordination issues slow production</li>
    <li>Margin is lost during execution—not estimating</li>
  </ul>
  <p>A structured system ensures:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Apples-to-apples bid comparisons</li>
    <li>Clear scope alignment across trades</li>
    <li>Fewer surprises during execution</li>
    <li>Better control of total job cost</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Common Failure Points</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Accepting bids without scope clarification</li>
    <li>Comparing lump sums without normalizing assumptions</li>
    <li>Missing exclusions that later become change orders</li>
    <li>No documentation of what was included</li>
    <li>Selecting vendors based on price alone</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>What a Structured System Looks Like</h2>
  <p>A MESLO-aligned subcontractor system includes:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Defined scope sheets for each trade</li>
    <li>Bid normalization across all proposals</li>
    <li>Clear inclusions/exclusions documented</li>
    <li>Leveling adjustments to compare true cost</li>
    <li>Vendor tracking based on performance</li>
  </ul>
  <p>This creates a system where:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>pricing is comparable</li>
    <li>scope is clear</li>
    <li>risk is visible</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>For CPAs and Advisors</h2>
  <p>Subcontractors are a major source of cost variance and margin leakage.</p>
  <p>A structured approach allows you to:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Identify where change orders originate</li>
    <li>Evaluate consistency across vendor pricing</li>
    <li>Validate whether estimates reflect full scope</li>
    <li>Improve job-level cost forecasting</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>How It Connects to MESLO</h2>
  <p>Subcontractors directly impact:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Materials → included vs excluded inputs</li>
    <li>Labor → overlap or duplication of scope</li>
    <li>Equipment → responsibility for tools and usage</li>
    <li>Overhead & margin → total job cost accuracy</li>
  </ul>
  <p>If subcontractor scope is unclear, the entire estimate becomes unstable.</p>

  <h2>Outcome</h2>
  <p>When subcontractors are structured correctly:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Bids are comparable and reliable</li>
    <li>Scope gaps are reduced</li>
    <li>Change orders decrease</li>
    <li>Margins are protected during execution</li>
  </ul>
