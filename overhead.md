<div class="container" style="padding:72px 0;">
  <h1 class="pageTitle">MESLO — Overhead</h1>
  <p class="lead">Capture true operating cost. Price with clarity. Protect margin.</p>

  <p>Overhead is where most businesses lose control. When it’s unclear or under-allocated, every job is mispriced.</p>
  <p>MESLO treats overhead as a defined, measurable system, not a percentage guess.</p>

  <h2>What “Overhead” Covers</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Office and administrative costs</li>
    <li>Salaries not tied to specific jobs</li>
    <li>Rent, utilities, and software</li>
    <li>Insurance, licenses, and compliance</li>
    <li>Vehicles, fuel, and general operations</li>
    <li>Owner compensation (non-production)</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Why It Matters</h2>
  <p>When overhead is not structured:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Bids look profitable—but aren’t</li>
    <li>True cost of doing business is hidden</li>
    <li>Profit gets confused with overhead</li>
    <li>Cash flow becomes inconsistent</li>
    <li>Growth increases risk instead of margin</li>
  </ul>
  <p>A structured system ensures:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Every job carries its share of overhead</li>
    <li>Pricing reflects real operating cost</li>
    <li>Profit is separated and visible</li>
    <li>Financial decisions are based on reality</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Common Failure Points</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Using arbitrary markup percentages</li>
    <li>Not separating overhead from profit</li>
    <li>Ignoring indirect costs in estimating</li>
    <li>No clear allocation method across jobs</li>
    <li>Treating overhead as “whatever is left”</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>What a Structured System Looks Like</h2>
  <p>A MESLO-aligned overhead system includes:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Defined overhead categories across the business</li>
    <li>Monthly cost baseline for operations</li>
    <li>Allocation method applied consistently to jobs</li>
    <li>Clear separation between overhead and profit</li>
    <li>Regular review tied to revenue and capacity</li>
  </ul>
  <p>This creates a system where:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>costs are fully captured</li>
    <li>pricing is accurate</li>
    <li>profit is intentional</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>For CPAs and Advisors</h2>
  <p>Overhead is the foundation of margin clarity.</p>
  <p>A structured approach allows you to:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Validate whether pricing covers full operating cost</li>
    <li>Identify gaps between revenue and true expense load</li>
    <li>Separate profit from overhead leakage</li>
    <li>Improve forecasting and financial reporting</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>How It Connects to MESLO</h2>
  <p>Overhead impacts everything:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Materials, labor, equipment, subcontractors → all depend on accurate total cost</li>
    <li>Pricing → determines whether jobs are truly profitable</li>
    <li>Cash flow → driven by coverage of fixed costs</li>
    <li>Growth → requires overhead scaling with control</li>
  </ul>
  <p>If overhead is unclear, margin is an illusion.</p>

  <h2>Outcome</h2>
  <p>When overhead is structured correctly:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Pricing reflects real business cost</li>
    <li>Profit becomes visible and consistent</li>
    <li>Cash flow stabilizes</li>
    <li>Growth becomes sustainable</li>
  </ul>
